<objective>
Close LEN-01: confirm `_convert_length_to_typst` as the single shared CSS-length -> Typst-length
helper and wire it into EVERY length-bearing site. The audit is closed and exhaustive (16-RESEARCH.md
verified via grep of docutils' own directive sources): only `image` (width/height — already wired),
`figure` (`:figwidth:` -> node["width"] — unwired), and `table`/`csv-table`/`list-table` (`:width:`
-> node["width"], all converging on the single `nodes.table` type — unwired) ever populate a
length-normalized attribute. So the work is exactly two new call sites (per D-03/D-03a), plus
fixtures proving both through a real compile.

Per D-03a both classes are in scope by definition: wiring previously-ignored figure/table lengths
IS the LEN-01 requirement ("reused at every length-bearing site"), not a new capability — reviewers
must not flag it as scope creep. Per D-03b there must remain exactly one conversion implementation;
no site may contain its own px/pc arithmetic, and the unsupported-unit -> drop-with-one-warning
contract (Phase 11 D-02) is preserved at every wired site.

Hard constraint (verified by a real failing compile this research cycle): Typst's `figure()` and
`table()` functions REJECT a direct `width:` argument — passing it aborts the whole compile. The
verified-working shape is wrapping the whole call: `block(width: X)[#figure(...)]` /
`block(width: X)[#table(...)]` (16-RESEARCH.md Pitfall 3). The `visit_image` kwarg pattern
(`, width: X` inside `image(...)`) must NOT be copied to figure/table.

Purpose: LEN-01 — one shared converter, consistently applied; kills the FIG-01 bug class everywhere.
Output: two wired call sites in translator.py, one extended + one new fixture, two new test classes.
</objective>
